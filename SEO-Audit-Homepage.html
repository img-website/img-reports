3. Schema / Structured Data

Detected (rich): WebSite (+SearchAction), @graph [Organization, WebSite, WebPage], FAQPage (DB), Service (18 serviceTypes), Corporation (logo + full sameAs). Best schema coverage on the site. @@context renders correctly.

HIGH 3.1 — Broken SearchAction target

The standalone WebSite node's SearchAction uses a literal placeholder:

"target": "{search_term_string}",
"query-input": "required name=search_term_string"
Solution: if the site has search, use a real template, e.g. "target": "https://www.imgglobalinfotech.com/search?q={search_term_string}". If there's no site search, remove the SearchAction entirely — a literal {search_term_string} as the URL is invalid and can suppress the sitelinks search box.
HIGH 3.2 — Entity duplication + empty Organization sameAs

The same company is described by an Organization node (in @graph, sameAs: [] empty) and a separate Corporation node (full sameAs with FB/X/IG/YouTube/LinkedIn). Two org-type entities + two WebSite nodes can fragment entity consolidation in Google's Knowledge Graph and AI engines.

Solution: consolidate to a single Organization node with the same @id referenced everywhere, populate its sameAs with the full social list, add logo, contactPoint, and address (you have India/USA/UAE/Germany offices — add them as location/address). Drop the redundant Corporation node or merge it in.
HIGH 3.3 — Missing Review / AggregateRating schema

The page has a "The Reviews are in" section + platform ratings, but no Review/AggregateRating markup.

Solution: add truthful AggregateRating + Review to the Organization node (use the genuine Clutch/Google rating + count). Strong rich-result + AI-trust signal.

7. Competitor Pages & Gaps

Competitive landscape (verified via web search): the high-ranking players for "mobile app development company India/USA" include Hyperlink InfoSystem, Appinventiv, TechAhead, The NineHertz, eSparkBiz, Mobulous, WillowTree, Intellectsoft. IMG Global Infotech does not appear in the top directory listicles for these head terms.

MEDIUM 7.1 — Build comparison & alternatives pages
Competitors capture high-intent traffic with "alternatives to [competitor]" and "X vs Y" pages. Create honest comparison pages (e.g., "IMG Global Infotech vs [competitor]", "best Appinventiv alternatives") with a feature/price/turnaround matrix. These rank well and convert bottom-funnel buyers.
MEDIUM 7.2 — Win the listicles
Most buyers find vendors through "top 10 app development companies" listicles (TheNineHertz, TopAppDevelopmentCompanies, DesignRush). Pursue inclusion/outreach + keep Clutch/GoodFirms profiles fully optimised (reviews, portfolio, verified data) — these listicles pull from those profiles.
LOW 7.3 — Differentiation on homepage
Competitors lead with hard proof (e.g., "4,500+ apps", "Clutch Global 2025 winner", named enterprise clients KFC/Adidas/IKEA). Surface your strongest verifiable proof points (1,200+ products, 10+ years, named brand clients) prominently and attributed.

MEDIUM 8.1 — Page-type alignment
Don't try to rank the homepage for "mobile app development company" — point that intent at the /mobile-app-development pillar (stronger topical match) and let the homepage own brand + "AI-driven digital transformation" positioning. Internally link accordingly.
MEDIUM 8.2 — Location-page thin-content / index-bloat risk
You have many "Mobile App Development in [country/city]" pages (India, USA, UK, Canada, Dubai, Australia, Kuwait, Saudi, Qatar, Italy, Netherland, Sweden, Spain, Germany, Singapore, Jaipur, Delhi…). If these are templated near-duplicates, they risk thin-content penalties and crawl-budget waste. Audit for unique local content (local case studies, addresses, testimonials) or consolidate/noindex the weakest.
LOW 8.3 — Conversion experience
Strong: clear CTAs, lead popup, exit-intent estimate popup, multi-office trust, WhatsApp. Ensure popups don't trigger intrusive-interstitial penalties on mobile (Google guideline) — keep them user-dismissible and not immediate on load.
