🎯 The short answer

The blogs are indexed and getting impressions — but almost every one sits at position 40–95 (page 4–9) for its target keyword, so it earns ~0 clicks. It's not a technical bug, not thin content, not the wrong page type. The real causes are (1) authority/age gap vs established competitors, (2) heavy keyword cannibalization — many of your own pages fight for the same term, (3) blogs are orphaned from your strong pages, and (4) some blogs rank for the wrong intent.

Hard proof: for "grocery delivery app development", 6 of your own pages compete (all stuck p65–87). For "apps like rapido" a blog ranks #1 but gets 0 clicks (wrong audience). And only 1 of your 100+ service pages links to any blog.

2 The REAL reasons (ranked, with evidence)

R1 Authority & age gap — new articles vs entrenched competitors in a money-keyword niche

Every blog targets a high-value commercial keyword ("taxi app development cost", "mobile app development company india"). Page 1 is owned by Appinventiv, Apptunix, Trangotech, Appscrip — articles that are 2–5 years old with backlinks earned to those specific URLs. Your articles are months old with ~no article-level links. Even where a competitor's DR is similar to yours (~60), their article age + links win.

Evidence (real GSC, last 3m): almost no blog has a single query in the "striking distance" zone (position 4–15). They are either deep (p40–95) or accidentally #1 for the wrong query. There is no "almost there" quick win — the gap is structural (time + links), not a tweak.

R3 Blogs are orphaned — no internal link equity reaches them

Authority flows through internal links. Audit of the whole codebase: /blog/ links appear in only 5 files — the 4 blog-system templates + exactly one service page (dating-app-development). So your 100+ high-authority service/money pages do not link to the blogs at all.

Effect: the blogs sit in an isolated silo, reachable mainly from the /blog listing and each other's "related posts". Google's internal PageRank barely reaches them → weak ranking power, regardless of content quality.

R4 Intent mismatch — some blogs rank #1 for the WRONG query

A few "app-like-X" blogs rank for consumer/navigational queries that bring zero business value:

  • #29 rapido ranks #1 for "apps like rapido", "rapido type apps" (497+491 impressions) — but those searchers want to download a bike-taxi app, not hire a developer. Result: ~0 clicks on those queries. The SERP there is Softonic / APKFab / app-store lists — a B2B cost guide doesn't belong.
  • #30 dream11 ranks #1 for "com dream11 app 2026 login" — people trying to log into Dream11, not build one.
Fix angle: retarget the title/H1/intro to unambiguous B2B intent (e.g. "Cost to Build a Bike-Taxi App Like Rapido in 2026 — Full Breakdown") so Google matches it to the commercial query that actually converts, not the consumer one.

R5 Stale freshness signals

For "cost" queries Google weights freshness (prices change yearly) — page-1 competitors put "2025/2026" in titles & refresh annually. Several of your blogs still say "in 2024" / "2023" (snapchat, educational, event-ticket, makemytrip). That reads as outdated.

4 What's missing & what to do (prioritized)

🌐 Website / content & SEO

  1. Kill cannibalization first (fastest win). Pick one owner page per keyword. For grocery: keep the best blog as the "cost/guide" page, 301 the weaker duplicates (incl. the -2023 listicle) into it, point the rest's internal links to the winner. Repeat for doctor, taxi, dating.
  2. Internal-link the blogs from your strong service pages — each service page links to its matching cost/guide blog with keyword anchor ("taxi app development cost breakdown"), and the blog links back to the service page CTA. This is the biggest untapped lever.
  3. Retarget the intent-mismatch blogs (rapido, dream11, snapchat, tinder): title/H1/intro → clear B2B "cost to build a … like X" framing so Google ranks them for commercial queries, not consumer ones.
  4. Refresh freshness: update "2024/2023" → "2026" in titles & content; add a visible "Updated 2026" + a current cost table where missing.
  5. Earn a few article-level backlinks (guest posts, digital-PR, niche directories) to the 3–4 highest-impression blogs (ecommerce-cost, taxi, dating, rapido) — this is what closes the authority gap competitors have.
  6. Build topical clusters: interlink all taxi posts together, all ecommerce posts together, with descriptive in-body anchors (not just sidebar widgets).

💻 Code (supports the above)

  1. Add a "Related service" CTA block in the blog template that links to the matching money page (and add blog links into service-page templates) — operationalises the internal-linking fix site-wide.
  2. author.url → real profile page (E-E-A-T; from prior reports).
  3. Remove HowTo schema + html_entity_decode() + add robots meta on DB blogs (from prior reports).
  4. Run responsecache:clear after edits.

Note: these code items are quality/plumbing — the ranking levers are on the content side (cannibalization, internal links, intent, freshness, links).

5 Bottom line

Your blogs are well-written, long, technically clean, and indexed — that's why this was confusing. They don't rank because of off-page & architecture factors, not the pages themselves:

  • Too many of your own pages chase the same keyword → split signals (grocery: 6 pages). Consolidate.
  • The blogs are walled off from your authority → only 1 service page links in. Connect them.
  • You're newer + less-linked than the entrenched page-1 guides → needs time + a few targeted backlinks.
  • A couple rank for consumer intent → retarget to B2B.

Order of impact: 1) de-cannibalize → 2) internal-link from service pages → 3) retarget intent + refresh year → 4) earn a few backlinks. Do these and the high-impression blogs (ecommerce-cost p42, taxi p39, dream11 p48) are the most likely to climb toward page 1 first.
