1. The root cause — why it has never ranked

The most important section.

CriticalOptimised for the brand "Zillow", not for a keyword you can win

ProblemTitle + H1 + OG all say Zillow – Redefining Real Estate with Digital Innovation. That's what Zillow's own site ranks for. An agency case study cannot outrank the actual product for its brand name. The commercial phrase only appears in the meta description ("Build your own real estate app like Zillow"), not in the title or H1.

WhyGoogle ranks on intent. "Zillow" searchers want the app; your buyers search "real estate app development", "build an app like Zillow", "Zillow clone app cost". None of those are in your title/H1, so you rank for nothing winnable.

FixRe-target to commercial intent:
Title → Zillow Like App Development | Build a Real Estate App Like Zillow — IMG Global Infotech
Visible H1 → Zillow Like App Development — Case Study. Keep "Zillow" as context; make "real estate app development / like app" the head term.

CriticalNear-duplicate template across 12 case studies → low uniqueness

ProblemSame skeleton, same 100+ logo "Cutting Edge Technologies" grid, same "AI-Powered Features" block, same "Proposed Team & Time" copy as Astrotalk, Uber, Netflix, Practo, etc. Only the nouns change. Even a decorative shape asset is the leftover practoPolygon1.svg from the Practo page.

WhyHelpful-Content and duplicate systems demote templated pages whose value is a swapped template; 12 lookalikes dilute each other so none becomes the authority for its topic.

FixMake ≥50% of the body unique to real estate: real build story, architecture (map/geo search, listings ingestion, valuation model, MLS/IDX feeds), screenshots, defensible outcomes. Push the shared logo grid to a secondary block.

2. Content / labels that do NOT belong on this page

You asked: kya is page ka content galat ya kisi aur page ka likha hai? Yes — multiple places.

CriticalThe hero image is labelled "Uber Eats" on a Zillow page

ProblemThe main hero image uses leftover Uber Eats labels:

![Uber Eats](…/zillowHeroImg.webp "Uber Eats")  — alt="Uber Eats", title="Uber Eats"

WhyThe alt/title of your most important (LCP) image describes a food-delivery brand, not real estate. Search engines and image search read alt text — this tells Google the hero is about "Uber Eats", scrambling topical relevance, and it proves the page was copied from the Uber Eats template without cleanup.

FixChange alt/title to something descriptive and on-topic, e.g. alt="Real estate app like Zillow — IMG Global Infotech".

HighFactual errors about Zillow

ProblemTwo wrong claims in "What is Zillow?":

"Zillow operates a database of over 135 million properties in Seattle, Washington."

(The 135M+ listings cover the whole US; Seattle is only the HQ.)

"Commercial real estate apps like Zillow help homeowners and tenants…"

(Zillow is residential real estate, not commercial — and the sentence then talks about homeowners/tenants, contradicting "commercial".)

WhyWrong facts about the very brand the page is built around destroy E-E-A-T and topical trust. It signals the content wasn't written or checked by someone who knows the domain.

FixCorrect: 135M+ properties across the U.S. (HQ in Seattle); Zillow is a residential real-estate marketplace. Verify every other stat (e.g. "100 Million", "31,000").

High"Key Result" admits its own numbers are hypothetical / negative

ProblemThe results block literally says:

"Bounce Rate is 35% — A hypothetical bounce rate of 35% suggests room for improvement in user engagement and website navigation."

Listed alongside "Over 100 Million", "More Than 31,000", "Zestimate Accuracy 80%".

WhyA case study's "Result" must be real, positive, and verifiable. Publishing a hypothetical stat that admits "room for improvement" is self-defeating — it tells prospects (and Google) the outcomes are invented placeholder text. It's the opposite of a proof point.

FixRemove the hypothetical/bounce-rate line. Replace the whole block with real, defensible outcomes (listings indexed, search latency, users, conversion, time-to-market). If confidential, use honest ranges — never "hypothetical".

MediumKeyword forced into admin-feature copy

Problem"Manage user accounts and permissions and resolve issues efficiently across the marketplace app like Zillow."

Why"marketplace app like Zillow" is bolted onto an admin sentence where it adds nothing — a minor keyword-stuffing tell that reads unnaturally.

FixWrite the feature plainly; place the keyword once where it fits naturally.

3. Content quality & humanisation

Your brief: real, organic, human content — verified.

HighAI-spun phrasing, rhetorical filler & an outright gibberish phrase

ProblemExamples copied from the live page:

"This investigation assesses the disruptive innovations that Zillow has leveraged…" / "In the real estate sector today, as an industry for research…"
Business Goal: "What does this imply? This signifies that… the ideal picture was how Zillow envisioned it; let us shift our focus toward how it was developed."
Project Challenges: "attaining a smooth and functional delay-effective interface… components of user customization and manipulation…"

Why"Investigation", "as an industry for research", "What does this imply?", "delay-effective", "manipulation" are classic AI-spinner / essay-generator output. This is exactly the low-effort, mass-produced pattern the Helpful-Content system demotes, and it reads as non-human to users.

FixRewrite in plain human English from the team that built it — what the client needed, what you decided, what you shipped. Kill the rhetorical filler and nonsense words.

4. Technical & on-page SEO

CriticalH1 is hidden and keyword-less

ProblemOnly H1 is <h1 class="hidden">Zillow - Case Study</h1>. The visible top is a logo image.

WhyThe H1 is a primary relevance signal; hiding it with a generic value wastes it and leaves no visible keyword-rich heading for users or crawlers.

FixOne visible H1 with the target phrase (Zillow Like App Development — Case Study) + a supporting line.

MediumFAQ schema injected, but no FAQ section on the page

Problem@include('tailwind.schema.faqSchema') runs, but no visible FAQ block renders (no DB FAQ rows for this URL).

WhyYou lose easy long-tail capture ("cost to build a real estate app like Zillow"), and FAQ structured data without matching visible content violates Google policy and risks a manual action.

FixAdd a real FAQ section (cost, timeline, features, MLS/IDX, monetisation) and populate the FAQ DB row so schema = content.

Low2 decorative images have no alt at all + wrong geo meta

ProblemTwo practoPolygon1.svg shapes (lines 217 & 1512) have no alt or title attribute. Also meta geo.region = DE (Germany) and the footer "Germany" office uses Canada flag assets — sitewide template leftovers.

WhyPurely decorative images should carry an explicit empty alt="" so screen readers skip them; a missing attribute can make them read the filename ("practoPolygon"). Wrong geo is a minor mis-signal. The "practo" filename on a Zillow page is more evidence of template copying.

FixAdd alt="" (and ideally rename the asset) to decorative shapes; set geo to the real market or remove it; fix the footer flag mapping sitewide.

MediumNo Article / CreativeWork / Service schema

ProblemOnly Breadcrumb + (empty) FAQ schema attempted; nothing describes the case study, author, client or service.

WhyRich structured data helps Google map agency → service → outcome and improves eligibility for enhanced results / AI citations.

FixAdd Article/CreativeWork (author + dates) and Service schema linking to your real-estate-app-development service page.

OKSelf-referencing canonical ✓ · INDEX, FOLLOW ✓ · allowed in robots.txt ✓ · in sitemap ✓ · title = OG title (consistent) ✓ · meta description has a commercial hook ✓ · OG + Twitter cards with image ✓ · 129 of 131 images have alt + title ✓.

5. Images

HighWrong alt on the hero + 2 missing alt + reused images

Problem(1) Hero alt/title = "Uber Eats" (see §2). (2) Two decorative shapes have no alt at all. (3) As on the other case studies, several admin-panel features likely reuse one graphic, and 131 images is a heavy DOM (mostly the shared logo grid).

WhyAlt text is a relevance + accessibility + image-SEO signal. A wrong hero alt mis-describes the page's main visual; missing alt hurts accessibility; image volume inflates payload and can hurt mobile LCP/INP.

FixFix the hero alt, add alt="" to decorative shapes, give each feature a unique image + alt, lazy-load below-the-fold, and trim the logo wall.

6. Internal & external linking

HighNo in-content link to a real-estate service/money page

ProblemIn-body, the only contextual links are to 4 other case studies. There is no link to your real-estate-app-development service page or a related cost blog from within the content (unlike the Uber page, which links to its taxi-app service page).

WhyInternal links pass relevance and authority and route users toward conversion. A deep case-study URL with no contextual links in or out is an island — little equity flows to it, and it passes none to your commercial pages.

Fix(1) Add contextual body links to your real-estate-app-development service + cost pages with descriptive anchors. (2) More importantly, link into this case study from that service page, related blogs and the case-study hub using anchors like "app like Zillow".

LowUnsourced facts & stats

Problem"135 million properties", "Over 100 Million", "31,000", "Zestimate Accuracy 80%" — none are sourced.

WhyUnsourced numbers read as invented and weaken trust/E-E-A-T, especially after the "hypothetical" admission above.

FixCite figures to named sources and verify them.

7. Is the URL user- & SEO-friendly?

Mostly yes/case-study/zillow-like-app

VerdictYes — clean, lowercase, hyphenated, short, readable, keyword present ("zillow-like-app"), no parameters/dates, and /case-study/ signals content type. Keep it.

NotePeople also search "real estate app like Zillow" / "Zillow clone" — capture those in the title, H1 and body, not the slug. Don't change the URL (a change forces redirects and risks losing equity).

8. UX

MediumLogo-as-hero, swipe-gated features, image-only Solution, duplicate forms

ProblemHero leads with the Zillow logo and no descriptive headline; features hide behind a "Swipe to show features" carousel; "Our Solution" / "Key Result" are image-led with little crawlable text; two near-identical lead forms + exit popup.

WhyLittle immediate text tells users/crawlers what the page offers; swipe-gated content is low-discoverability on desktop and for crawlers; multiple forms + popup raise interaction cost (INP) and feel pushy.

FixAdd a clear text headline + sub-line + primary CTA in the hero; expose features as crawlable text; make Solution/Result text-first; keep one well-placed form.
