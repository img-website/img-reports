🛡️ Why this page has never ranked — root causes (WHY + FIX)

1. H1 says "Application", title / URL / OG all say "App" — keyword fragmentationCritical
Why: Hero H1 is "Insurance Application Development Company" but every other surface uses "Insurance App Development Company". "App" and "Application" are not synonymous to Google's keyword index — they map to different search-volume buckets. The page is competing with itself: half of its topical weight goes to "application" (low-volume) and half to "app" (high-volume). On a site where one phrasing already loses to dozens of competitors, splitting the signal is fatal.
Fix: Change H1 to "Insurance App Development Company for Insurers, Brokers & InsurTech Startups". Mention "application" once in body as a synonym, but don't use it as the primary anchor.
2. Top-tier YMYL Insurance with zero IRDAI / regulatory contentCritical
Why: Insurance sits at the absolute top of YMYL — money, health, contracts all in one. Google demands the strictest possible expertise signals before ranking a service page in this niche. A buyer looking for "insurance app development company" expects to see depth on:
  • IRDAI (Insurance Regulatory and Development Authority of India) registration, sandbox, e-Insurance Account, Bima Sugam framework, Insurance Acts (1938, IRDA Act 1999)
  • Global regulators — NAIC (US), Solvency II (EU), PRA / FCA (UK), MAS (Singapore), APRA (Australia)
  • Standards — IFRS 17 / Ind AS 117, HIPAA (US health), GDPR, India DPDP Act 2023, PCI-DSS for payments
  • Insurer integrations — Acko, Digit, PolicyBazaar, PhonePe Insurance, Bajaj Allianz, HDFC ERGO, ICICI Lombard, Tata AIG, Star Health, Lemonade, Root, Hippo
  • Claim & underwriting tech — image-OCR claim intake, fraud-graph ML, telematics (TSP) APIs, IoT for property/health, NLP claim adjudication
  • Bima Sugam (India's open insurance marketplace) — building for it is a major buyer-search trend in 2026
The page mentions none. Without this depth, the URL's maximum possible rank is capped well below page 1.
Fix: Add a dedicated H2 "IRDAI & Global Insurance Compliance We Build For" — 800-1,200 words covering each regulator and standard. Then a "Insurer & InsurTech Integrations" H2 with named partners and APIs.
3. 11 off-topic case studies — sitewide template blockCritical
Why: Same offending block as your other URLs: Blinkit (grocery), Astrotalk (astrology), Zillow (real estate), Uber, Tinder, TikTok, MoneyLion (banking — only fintech-adjacent), TaskRabbit, UberEats, Netflix, Practo. Zero insurance case studies. On a YMYL Insurance page Google reads this and concludes "this is a generic app shop, not an insurance specialist". The case-study URLs use the /case-study/{brand}-like-app clone-app pattern — additional algorithmic-quality demotion.
Fix: Delete the entire off-topic block from this URL. Replace with 3-5 real or labeled-concept insurance projects: a motor-insurance app with TSP integration, a health-insurance claim-intake app, a Bima Sugam plug-in, a SME group-insurance dashboard, a parametric crop-insurance prototype.

Leading space before https:// — invalid URL per RFC 3986. Google's schema parser drops the WebPage node. Likely a sitewide template bug.
