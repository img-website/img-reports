HIGH 2.1 — Three-way geo conflict (India vs USA vs Global)

Title/meta/H1 say India; both schema descriptions say USA; twitter says globally. This is the page's biggest on-page issue — it gives Google and AI engines contradictory location signals for the same entity.

Solution: pick one primary market and make it consistent across title, meta, H1, and both schema descriptions. If India is the SEO priority (matches title/H1), update the two schema descriptions from "in the USA" → "in India" (or "in India & USA"). Don't leave USA in schema while the title says India.
LOW 2.2 — og:title vs twitter:title differ
og: "Best iOS App Development Company"; twitter: "Top-Rated iOS App Development Company". Harmonise for consistent social sharing.
MEDIUM 2.3 — Keyword targeting

Primary "iOS app development company" well-placed. Strong secondary: "iPhone app development", "custom iOS app development", "hire iOS app developers". Opportunity terms (confirm volume in a tool): "iOS app development cost" (answered in FAQ — promote to a visible block), "iPad app development", "iOS app development company in USA".

Solution: surface a citable on-page "cost" block; reinforce the "hire iOS app developers" internal-link anchor (currently linked once).
COSMETIC 2.4 — Heading casing
H2 "Latest Cutting edge technologies we use" → "Latest Cutting-Edge Technologies We Use".

3. Schema / Structured Data

Present (verified): Organization + WebSite + WebPage (@graph), Service, BreadcrumbList (DB-driven), FAQPage (DB-driven, 8 FAQs live). @@context renders correctly — not a bug.

HIGH 3.1 — Missing Review / AggregateRating schema

Page visibly shows "4.5/5 Average Rating" + 6 named testimonials, but no review structured data. Biggest rich-result + AI-trust opportunity.

"aggregateRating": {
  "@type": "AggregateRating",
  "ratingValue": "4.5",
  "reviewCount": "6",
  "bestRating": "5"
}
Solution: attach AggregateRating + Review to the Service/Organization node. Only genuine on-page reviews; truthful counts.
LOW-MED 3.2 — Organization sameAs empty in @graph
First Organization node has "sameAs": [] while Service node lists all socials. Populate Organization sameAs (FB, X, IG, LinkedIn, YouTube).
LOW 3.3 — Mixed URL style in schema
WebPage/Service use hard-coded full URLs while other nodes use {{asset()}}. Works, but standardise on asset() for staging safety. Also consider areaServed: ["India","United States","Global"].

6. Content Quality (Organic vs AI, E-E-A-T)

MEDIUM 6.1 — AI-flavoured / keyword-stuffed intro

Hero copy: "…optimized for performance in ios app development services. Understand everything you need to know about development in accordance with Apple guidelines… by iphone app developers." Reads machine-generated and stuffs the keyword; also "ios" / "iphone" are lowercase mid-sentence (should be iOS / iPhone).

Solution: rewrite into specific, human claims with proof (e.g., "We've shipped 1,200+ apps over 10+ years, including App Store-featured iOS apps…"). Fix iOS/iPhone capitalisation throughout.
MEDIUM 6.2 — Thin authority signals (E-E-A-T)
No author/reviewer byline or visible "last updated". Add a credibility line + surface the schema dateModified (currently 2024-11-13) as a visible date — update honestly when revised.
LOW-MED 6.3 — Unattributed stats
"4.5/5", testimonials — attribute to a source ("4.5/5 from 30 reviews on Clutch"). Verifiable claims rank + cite better.
POSITIVE 6.4 — FAQ strong
8 real FAQs (cost, platforms, timeline, process, top apps, native vs hybrid, integrations, hiring) with FAQPage schema. Keep answers self-contained (40–60 words).
