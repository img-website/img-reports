💊 Why this page has never ranked — root causes (WHY + FIX)

1. Hero subtitle has a brand typo — "IMG Global Inotech"Critical
Why: Hero body copy (line 144 of blade) says "Connect with IMG Global Inotech, a leading medicine delivery app development company..." — missing the "f". A typo of your own brand name in the LCP-adjacent hero copy is the strongest possible "this page is templated and never QAd" signal Google's Helpful Content System can detect. Same family of bug as the "Comapny" alt-text on the online-exam page.
Fix: Change "Inotech" → "Infotech" on line 144. Add a sitewide build-time spell-check for the words IMG Global Infotech / IMGGlobalInfotech to prevent recurrence.
2. Four different head-titles for one URL (worst-of-six audited)Critical
Why: No other page on the site has four different adjectives splitting the topical signal this badly:
SurfacePhrase
<title>Medicine Delivery App Development Company | IMG Global Infotech
og:titleOn-Demand Medicine Delivery App Development Company
twitter:titleCustom Medicine Delivery App Development Company
H1Top Medicine Delivery App Development Company
Title also brand-stuffs "| IMG Global Infotech" (wastes ~25 chars of keyword space). The visible H1 says "Top", the OG card on social posts says "On-Demand", the Twitter card says "Custom" — every channel sends a different topical signal.
Fix: Pick one phrase, use everywhere. Recommended primary title (58 chars):
Medicine Delivery App Development Company — Pharmacy & Rx
3. 11 off-topic case studies — same sitewide template blockCritical
Why: "Case Studies Inspiring Innovation" lists Blinkit, Astrotalk, Zillow, Uber, Tinder, TikTok, MoneyLion, TaskRabbit, UberEats, Netflix, Practo. None are medicine-delivery apps. (Practo is closest — telemedicine — but not pharmacy.) On a regulated pharmacy URL Google reads this and decides "generic app shop, not a pharmacy specialist". Same template block dragging down every page on the site.
Fix: Delete from this URL. Replace with 3-5 real or labeled-concept pharmacy/medicine projects: a 1mg-style Rx-upload app, a multi-vendor pharmacy marketplace, a chronic-care subscription pillbox app, a B2B distributor (DPCO-priced) app, a hospital pharmacy management module.
4. Massive keyword-stuffed legacy meta tags (this page has the worst)Critical
Why: Other pages on the site have legacy meta bloat (revisit-after, distribution, etc.) — but this URL also adds abstract, summary, owner, pagename, category, subtitle. The category tag is a 14-keyword stuffing: 
"Medicine Delivery App Development, Healthcare App Solutions, On-Demand Delivery Services, telemedicine, telemedicine app, medicine delivery, online medicine delivery, online medicine delivery app apps, online medicine delivery app near me, on demand delivery services, medicine ordering app, medicine app, pharmacy delivery app, pharmacy development"
Note "app apps" (typo) and "app near me" (local-intent term stuffed into a global page). The owner tag is worse — it says "Transfer your unique Pharmacy deliver app like tata 1MG idea to reality..." — typo + competitor brand piggyback. These tags don't help SEO any more, but they actively flag the head as "templated, never-audited spam attempt" for any classifier that does look.
Fix: Delete abstract, summary, owner, pagename, category, subtitle from the blade entirely. Plus the sitewide legacy tags (revisit-after, distribution, coverage, rating, language, copyright, rights, reply-to, web-author) at the layout level.
5. Brand piggyback in meta description + body — "1mg / Netmeds / tata 1MG / Replica"Critical
Why:
  • Meta description: "Get custom pharmacy app solutions like 1mg or Netmeds and start an online medicine business today!"
  • owner meta: "pharmacy deliver app like tata 1MG"
  • H2 below the fold: "Expertise to Build Custom Medicine App Replica"
"Replica" = clone. Tata 1mg, Netmeds (Reliance), Apollo Pharmacy all have active legal teams that file regular take-downs. Google's algorithm increasingly demotes pages that piggyback competitor brand SERPs.
Fix: Rewrite meta description without competitor brands. Rename H2 from "Replica" → "Apps inspired by leading pharmacy platforms". Remove the offending owner meta entirely.
6. Self-serving aggregateRating 4.9/140 in Product schemaCritical
Why: Same self-serving reviews pattern. No visible review widget on page. Plus Product is wrong type for a B2B service.
Fix: Delete the Product schema block (lines 5-23).
7. Zero CDSCO / DPCO / Schedule-H / Rx-verification contentCritical
Why: Medicine delivery is a regulated, YMYL-adjacent niche. Buyers expect:
  • CDSCO (Central Drugs Standard Control Organisation, India)
  • DPCO / NPPA price controls (Drug Price Control Order)
  • Schedule H / H1 / X prescription drugs — verification flow, e-prescription rules
  • State Pharmacy Council registration of dispensing pharmacist
  • DSCSA (US Drug Supply Chain Security Act) — track & trace
  • DEA scheduling (US) — controlled substances
  • HIPAA, GDPR, India DPDP Act 2023
  • FDA 21 CFR Part 11 for e-records
  • Rx-verification integrations — Pharmacy First, India Pharmacy registers, RxNorm, FHIR Medications
Page mentions HIPAA only as a one-line tile. None of the others. Without this depth, the URL's maximum rank is capped well below page 1.
Fix: Add a dedicated H2 "Pharmacy & Drug Compliance We Build For" — 800-1200 words covering each regulator and standard. Then an "Rx Verification & Pharmacy Workflow Integrations" H2 with named systems.
8. Typos in H3 tile headings — "Ai Powered" / "Eprescription"High
Why: Two H3 tiles in the "High-Tech Medicine Delivery App Development Solutions" section have casing/spelling slips:
  • "Ai Powered Medicine Ordering Setups" — should be "AI-Powered". "Setups" is also an odd word; "platforms" or "workflows" is more natural.
  • "Secure Eprescription Management Systems" — should be "E-Prescription" (or "Electronic Prescription"). "Eprescription" is not a recognised industry term.
Both are visible to Google's NLP and to human Quality Raters as low-effort errors. They also break exact-match for related queries (people search "e-prescription", "ai-powered", not "eprescription" / "ai powered").
Fix: Change to "AI-Powered Medicine Ordering Workflows" and "Secure E-Prescription Management Systems".
9. og:description has a leading spaceMedium
Why: <meta property="og:description" content=" As a leading... — leading space inside the value. Most social parsers trim, but some Telegram / Discord clients display the literal " As..." with the space. Indicates the template wasn't audited.
Fix: Remove leading space (line 118 of blade).
10. Schema "name" stuffs the brand into the titleHigh
Why: Product, WebPage, and Service schemas all use "name": "Medicine Delivery App Development Company | IMG Global Infotech" — pipe-stuffing the brand into the structured-data name. Google reads the schema name as the canonical entity title, and pipe-delimited brand suffix in name field is a known low-quality pattern.
Fix: Change all schema name values to "Medicine Delivery App Development Company" (without the brand pipe). Add a sitewide spell-check rule to prevent the pipe-stuff pattern.
11. Brand-string inconsistency in schemasMedium
Why: Organization name "IMGGlobalInfotech" (no spaces) ❌ — the rest of the site has "IMG Global Infotech". The page's sameAs at least uses the correct x.com/imgglobal URL ✅ (one of the few audited pages with this right).
Fix: Standardise on "IMG Global Infotech" (with spaces) across all schema blocks.
12. H2 "Expertise to Build Custom Medicine App Replica"High
Why: "Replica" is the clone-app pattern. On a YMYL-adjacent page this looks worse — buyers (CTO of pharmacy chain, pharma D2C founder) don't want a "replica"; they want a compliant differentiated product.
Fix: Rename "Apps inspired by leading pharmacy platforms" (with clear separation from your real case studies).
13. H2 "Why Select IMG Global Infotech for Medicine Delivery App Services?"Medium
Why: "Why Select" reads awkwardly in English; "Why Choose" is standard. Same non-native phrasing as appears on other pages in the site — signals "templated, never edited".
Fix: Change to "Why Choose IMG Global Infotech for Medicine Delivery App Services".
14. Off-topic "Other On-Demand…" cross-linkingHigh
Why: Final block lists 8 unrelated apps — Healthcare (relevant), Taxi (not), Food Delivery (not), Beauty/Salon (not), Laundry (not), Car Wash (not), Doctor on Demand (relevant), Grocery (not). Most send pharmacy-intent traffic outward to unrelated topics.
Fix: Replace with related pharma/health pages: /healthcare-app-development, /doctor-on-demand-app-development, /hospital-management-software, /diet-and-nutrition-app-development, /fitness-app-development, plus a new /pharmacy-pos-software if you build it.
