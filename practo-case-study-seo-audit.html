1. The root cause — why it has never ranked

Most important section.

CriticalOptimised for the brand "Practo", not a keyword you can win

ProblemTitle + H1 + OG all say Practo – Revolutionizing Digital Healthcare Solutions. That's what Practo's own site ranks for. An agency case study can't outrank the actual product for its brand name. The commercial phrase ("healthcare app like Practo") only sits in the meta description.

WhyGoogle ranks on intent. "Practo" searchers want the app; your buyers search "healthcare app development", "build an app like Practo", "telemedicine app development cost". None are in the title/H1, so you rank for nothing winnable.

FixRe-target to commercial intent:
Title → Practo Like App Development | Build a Healthcare App Like Practo — IMG Global Infotech
Visible H1 → Practo Like App / Healthcare App Development — Case Study. Keep "Practo" as context; make "healthcare/telemedicine app development" the head term.

CriticalNear-duplicate template across 12 case studies

ProblemSame skeleton, same shared "Cutting Edge Technologies" logo grid + "AI-Powered Features" block + "Proposed Team & Time" copy as Astrotalk, Uber, Zillow, Tinder, etc. (This page is in fact the source of the leftover practoPolygon/Rectangle/Ellipse shapes that show up on the other case studies.)

WhyHelpful-Content and duplicate systems demote templated pages whose value is a swapped template; 12 lookalikes dilute each other so none becomes the authority for its topic.

FixMake ≥50% of the body unique to healthcare/telemedicine: real build story, architecture (appointment engine, e-prescription, video consults, HIPAA/DISHA-style data security, EHR integration), screenshots, defensible outcomes. Push the shared logo grid to a secondary block.

2. Errors that hurt extra on a healthcare (YMYL) page

These are exactly the "wrong content / content that doesn't belong" items you asked about.

Critical"Patent App" heading — should be "Patient App"

ProblemThe feature group heading reads ### Patent App instead of Patient App (a "patent" is an invention right — nothing to do with healthcare).

WhyIt's a visible H3 on a healthcare page. On a YMYL topic, a basic medical-term error in a heading is a strong low-quality / low-expertise signal to both users and Google, and it undercuts the whole "we build healthcare apps" claim.

FixChange to "Patient App". Proofread every heading on the page.

HighGibberish keyword injection + a stray-character typo

ProblemIn "What is Practo?":

"In the era of technology, the IoT-enabled health monitoring app development in charge of the app also has a novel approach to healthcare."

And in a feature line: "Facilitate the booking, rescheduling, and 2cancellation of appointments…" (stray "2").

Why"IoT-enabled health monitoring app development in charge of the app" is a keyword phrase jammed into a sentence where it has no grammatical role — classic stuffing/spinning. "2cancellation" is an obvious un-proofread typo. Both scream low-effort, machine-assisted content on a page that needs to look authoritative.

FixRewrite the sentence in plain English (drop the jammed keyword); fix "2cancellation". Full proofread pass.

HighCompetitor "Doctolib" injected into a Practo feature

ProblemDoctor-app feature: "Make video calls and consult with patients in a secure communication system with an app like Doctolib."

WhyDoctolib is a different healthcare brand; dropping it into a Practo feature is off-topic keyword injection that confuses the page's focus and reads as spam — same pattern as the Rapido/Bolt/Ola injections on the Uber page.

FixRemove "an app like Doctolib" from the Practo feature. If you want to target "app like Doctolib", build a dedicated page.

HighContradictory / unsourced stats

ProblemThe "What is Practo?" block says 1L+ (100,000) Doctors and "50K+ Hospitals"; the "Key Result" block says 200,000+ Verified Doctors, "50 Million+ Downloads", "10 Million+ Consultations". The doctor count contradicts itself, and none are sourced.

WhyTwo different numbers for the same metric on one page is a clear accuracy/trust failure — heightened on YMYL. It also blurs attribution: these read as Practo's own corporate figures presented as "the result of the case study", implying you built Practo.

FixMake the numbers consistent, cite the source, and clearly separate "About Practo (context)" stats from "What our client achieved" results. Don't present Practo's brand numbers as your project's outcome.

3. Content quality, humanisation & E-E-A-T

Your brief: real, organic, human content — verified. On YMYL, expertise matters most.

HighNo author / medical reviewer — and AI-spun phrasing

ProblemNo author byline, credentials, or "medically reviewed by" marker. Phrasing is spun, e.g. "Practo has become a fully-fledged telemedicine app development solution" (Practo is a healthcare platform, not a dev solution), and the rhetorical "Still, the initial idea of the start-up was completely different…".

WhyFor health content Google weighs E-E-A-T heavily; an anonymous, machine-flavoured page about medical services lacks the trust signals it expects, which suppresses ranking. Mislabelling Practo also reads as non-expert.

FixAdd a named author/expert box (and ideally a reviewer), rewrite in plain human English, and correct domain terms.

4. Missing & weak sections

MediumFAQ schema injected, but no FAQ section on the page

Problem@include('tailwind.schema.faqSchema') runs, but no visible FAQ block renders (no DB FAQ rows for this URL).

WhyYou lose easy long-tail capture ("cost to build a healthcare app like Practo", "telemedicine app development cost"), AND FAQ structured data with no matching visible content violates Google policy and risks a manual action — and FAQ rich results are actually best-suited to health/medical pages like this one.

FixAdd a real FAQ section (cost, timeline, features, compliance/data-security, monetisation) and populate the FAQ DB row so schema = content.

MediumNo testimonial, no process; image-led Solution; no "Business Goal" section

ProblemNo client quote, no process/approach narrative; "Our Solution" is image-led; and unlike the other case studies this one has no explicit "Business Goal" section (jumps from "What is Practo?" to "Project Ideation").

WhyTrust + structure signals matter for commercial/health content; image-led sections add little crawlable text; a missing goal section weakens the case-study narrative.

FixAdd a Business Goal section, a process/approach block, a testimonial, and convert Solution into real text.

5. Technical & on-page SEO

CriticalH1 is hidden and keyword-less

ProblemOnly H1 is <h1 class="hidden">Practo - Case Study</h1>. The visible top is a logo image.

WhyThe H1 is a primary relevance signal; hiding it with a generic value wastes it and leaves no visible keyword-rich heading.

FixOne visible H1 with the target phrase (Practo Like App / Healthcare App Development — Case Study) + a supporting line.

MediumNo Article / CreativeWork / Service / MedicalBusiness schema

ProblemOnly Breadcrumb + (empty) FAQ schema attempted; nothing describes the case study, author, client or service.

WhyRich structured data helps Google map agency → service → outcome; on a health page, author + organisation schema also reinforce E-E-A-T.

FixAdd Article/CreativeWork (author + dates) and Service schema linking to your healthcare-app-development service page.

LowWrong geo meta

Problemmeta geo.region = DE (Germany) on a global page — sitewide template leftover seen on all case studies.

WhyMinor wrong geo mis-signal.

FixSet geo to the intended market or remove it (sitewide).

PassWhat is already correct

OKSelf-referencing canonical ✓ · INDEX, FOLLOW ✓ · allowed in robots.txt ✓ · in sitemap ✓ · title = OG title (consistent) ✓ · meta description has a commercial hook ✓ · OG + Twitter cards with image ✓ · "Key Result" has quantified numbers ✓ · 128 of 137 content images have alt + title ✓.

6. Images

Medium9 decorative shapes have no alt at all + heavy image count

ProblemNine decorative SVG shapes (practoPolygon1/2/3, practoRectangle1, practoEllipse1) have no alt or title attribute. 137 images total is the heaviest of the case studies; admin features likely reuse one graphic.

WhyDecorative images should carry an explicit empty alt="" so screen readers skip them; a missing attribute can make them read the filename. The image volume inflates payload and can hurt mobile LCP/INP.

FixAdd alt="" to decorative shapes, give each feature a unique image + alt, lazy-load below-the-fold (already lazy on these), and trim the logo wall.

7. Internal & external linking

HighNo in-content link to a healthcare-app service/money page

ProblemIn-body contextual links go only to 4 other case studies. There's a "Latest Insights" blog block, but no contextual link to your healthcare/telemedicine app-development service or cost page from within the unique content.

WhyInternal links pass relevance and authority and route users toward conversion. A deep case-study URL with no contextual links in or out is an island — little equity reaches it, and it passes none to your commercial pages.

Fix(1) Add contextual body links to your healthcare-app-development service + cost pages with descriptive anchors. (2) Link into this case study from that service page, related blogs and the case-study hub using anchors like "app like Practo".

8. Is the URL user- & SEO-friendly?

Mostly yes/case-study/practo-like-app

VerdictYes — clean, lowercase, hyphenated, short, readable, keyword present ("practo-like-app"), no parameters/dates, and /case-study/ signals content type. Keep it.

NotePeople also search "healthcare app like Practo" / "telemedicine app" / "Practo clone" — capture those in the title, H1 and body, not the slug. Don't change the URL (a change forces redirects and risks losing equity).

9. UX

MediumLogo-as-hero, swipe-gated features, image-led Solution, duplicate forms

ProblemHero leads with the Practo logo and no descriptive headline; features hide behind a "Swipe to show features" carousel; "Our Solution" is image-led; two near-identical lead forms + exit popup.

WhyLittle immediate text tells users/crawlers what the page offers; swipe-gated content is low-discoverability; multiple forms + popup raise interaction cost (INP) and feel pushy.

FixAdd a clear text headline + sub-line + primary CTA in the hero; expose features as crawlable text; make Solution text-first; keep one well-placed form.
