1. The root cause — why it has never ranked

The single most important section. Everything else is supporting detail.

CriticalSearch-intent & keyword mismatch — you are competing with Astrotalk for its own name

ProblemTitle, H1 and meta all target the brand: Astrotalk – Connecting Users with Expert Astrologers Online. That is what Astrotalk's own app/website ranks for. A web-development agency case study cannot outrank the actual product for its brand name.

WhyGoogle matches pages to intent. Anyone typing "Astrotalk" wants the app, not an agency. Your real buyers search commercial-intent phrases like "astrotalk clone app development", "build an app like astrotalk", "astrology app development cost", "astrotalk like app development company". None of those phrases appear in your title, H1, or first paragraph — so you rank for nothing you can actually win.

FixRe-target the page to commercial intent (organic, no keyword stuffing). Example direction:
Title → Astrotalk Like App Development | Build Your Own Astrology App — IMG Global Infotech
H1 (visible) → Astrotalk Like App Development — Case Study
First 100 words should naturally contain "develop an app like Astrotalk", "astrology app development", "astrologer consultation app". Keep "Astrotalk" as context, make "like app development" the head term.

CriticalNear-duplicate template across 12 case studies → low uniqueness / "thin + boilerplate"

ProblemThis page shares the same skeleton, the same "Cutting Edge Technologies" 100+ logo block, the same "AI-Powered Features" section and the same "Proposed Team / Timeline" copy as Uber, Tinder, Netflix, Blinkit, MoneyLion etc. Only the nouns change.

WhyGoogle's Helpful-Content and duplicate-detection systems demote pages whose primary value is a swapped template. When 12 URLs look 80% identical, none of them is treated as the authoritative answer for its topic — they dilute each other.

FixMake at least 50% of the body genuinely unique to astrology: real problem→solution narrative, specific architecture decisions (real-time chat scale, astrologer payout logic, kundli/horoscope engine, queue management), screenshots, measurable outcomes. Move the giant shared tech-logo grid into a collapsed/secondary block so the unique astrology content dominates.

2. Technical & on-page SEO

Meta, headings, index/follow, canonical, schema.

CriticalH1 is hidden and carries no keyword

ProblemThe only live H1 is <h1 class="hidden">Astrotalk - Case Study</h1> (visually hidden). The big visible "heading" at the top is just the Astrotalk logo image. A second keyword H1 in the file is commented out.

WhyThe H1 is one of the strongest on-page relevance signals. Hiding it with display:none and giving it a generic, keyword-free value wastes that signal, and a visually-hidden H1 can read as a manipulation pattern. There is no visible, keyword-rich primary heading for the user or the crawler.

FixMake one visible H1 with the target phrase, e.g. Astrotalk Like App Development with a supporting line. Keep the logo as decorative, not as the heading.

HighTitle & meta description sell Astrotalk, not your service

ProblemMeta description: "Explore how Astrotalk became a leading astrology platform…" — it markets Astrotalk, with the agency offer buried mid-sentence.

WhyTitle + meta are your SERP advert and a ranking/CTR input. Leading with the brand wastes the most valuable pixels on a term you can't win and gives no commercial hook ("cost", "features", "build your own").

FixRewrite to lead with the service + a benefit + a CTA verb, e.g. "Want to build an app like Astrotalk? See how we developed a scalable astrology consultation platform — features, tech stack & timeline. Get a free quote." Keep it ≤155 chars.

MediumFAQ schema is injected but there is NO FAQ section on the page

Problem@include('tailwind.schema.faqSchema') runs, but the rendered page shows no visible Frequently-Asked-Questions block (the DB has no FAQ rows for this URL, so the schema outputs nothing useful — and even if it did, there would be schema with no matching visible content).

WhyTwo losses: (1) you miss the FAQ content itself, which is the easiest way to capture long-tail queries like "how much does it cost to build an app like astrotalk"; (2) FAQ structured data without matching on-page content violates Google's structured-data policy and can trigger a manual action.

FixAdd a real, visible FAQ section (6–10 genuine questions about cost, timeline, features, tech, monetisation) AND populate the FAQ DB row for this URL so schema and content match.

MediumNo CaseStudy / Article / Service schema — only FAQ & Breadcrumb attempted

ProblemThe page is a case study but exposes no Article, CreativeWork or Service/Organization structured data describing what was built, by whom, the client, the outcome.

WhyRich structured data helps Google understand entity relationships (agency → service → client outcome) and improves eligibility for enhanced results and AI-overview citations.

FixAdd Article or CreativeWork schema with author (E-E-A-T), datePublished/Modified, plus Service linking back to your astrology-app-development service page.

LowStray / wrong meta values from the shared template

Problemmeta geo.region = DE (Germany) on an India/global astrology page; apple-mobile-web-app-title = "My App" (default placeholder); footer "Germany" office uses Canada flag images (canadaFlag.webp, hq-canada.webp).

WhyIndividually minor, but wrong geo signals and placeholder values are sloppy-quality signals and can mildly confuse geo-targeting. The flag mismatch is a sitewide footer bug, not unique to this page.

FixSet geo to the intended market (or remove it), replace the "My App" placeholder, and fix the footer flag asset mapping sitewide.

PassWhat is already correct

OKSelf-referencing canonical ✓ · INDEX, FOLLOW in production ✓ · allowed in robots.txt ✓ · in sitemap.xml ✓ · OG + Twitter cards complete with image ✓ · valid viewport/mobile metas ✓ · every <img> has both alt and title ✓.

3. Content quality, keywords & relevance

Your brief: real organic, humanised content — verified. This is where the page is weakest.

CriticalContent is AI-spun, not human — with broken, embarrassing sentences

ProblemSeveral lines are clearly machine-spun and make no sense. Real examples copied from the live page:

"Intelligent suggestions to ensure the appropriate astrologer rates psychos according to their choice and requirements."
"Astrotalk is one of the fastest-growing app developments that connects millions of users…" (should be "platforms")
"Facile registration…", "favorable proportions of margins purported for astrologers", "control their availability rate", "Monitor all transactions and Layouts".

WhyThis is exactly the "unhelpful, automatically-generated" signal Google's spam & Helpful-Content systems target. It also destroys E-E-A-T and human trust — a prospect who reads "rates psychos" will not hire you. Spun synonyms ("Facile", "purported") are the classic fingerprint of article-spinner output.

FixRewrite every section in plain, human English by someone who knows the project. Short sentences, concrete detail, no thesaurus-spinning. This single change lifts both quality signals and conversion.

HighFactual errors that undermine credibility

Problem(a) About section says astrologers give advice "for free" — Astrotalk is a paid per-minute consultation model. (b) Random competitor "Panditji" is dropped in with no context. (c) In the "Result" block, Bounce Rate is 65.39% is listed as a positive achievement.

WhyA 65% bounce rate is a problem metric, not a success — showcasing it as a result signals the content was assembled without understanding. False "free" claims and irrelevant brand mentions reduce topical trust and can mislead users.

FixRemove the "free" claim, drop the unexplained "Panditji" mention, and replace the bounce-rate "win" with genuine positive outcomes (retention, consultations completed, repeat-user %, revenue growth) that you can defend.

HighLeftover content from the Netflix template (proof of copy-paste)

ProblemThe blade still contains commented-out Netflix copy: "See What's Next … Astrotalk is another name for entertainment as the world's top OTT app … keep users binge-watching their favorite zoner." It is commented (not rendered), but it proves the page was duplicated from the Netflix case study rather than written for astrology.

WhyEven commented, it confirms the duplication problem from §1; and a stray paste like this is one edit away from going live. It explains the generic feel Google is reacting to.

FixDelete the dead Netflix block and audit the file for any other carried-over text. Treat each case study as a unique asset, not a clone.

HighThin unique body — and a section that does not belong

ProblemThe genuinely unique astrology text is roughly 6 short paragraphs (About, Goal, Idea, Challenges + feature one-liners). The rest is a 100+ logo "Cutting Edge Technologies" grid and a generic "AI-Powered Features" block reused on every case study — large in size, near-zero unique value here.

WhyWord-count isn't the goal, but unique depth is. A page that is mostly shared boilerplate + logo walls gives Google little reason to see it as the best result for "astrotalk like app". The AI-features section is generic filler, not specific to what you actually built.

FixExpand the unique astrology narrative (the build story, decisions, results). Shrink/secondary-rank the shared tech grid. Either make the AI section project-specific (what AI you actually shipped) or remove it.

MediumMissing sections a ranking case study needs

ProblemNo visible FAQ, no client testimonial/quote, no development process/approach narrative, no author/expert byline (E-E-A-T), no clear cost/timeline-to-build hook, and the "Our Solution"/"Result" are single images with little crawlable text.

WhyThese are the sections that capture long-tail queries, build trust, and give Google extractable text. Image-only "Solution/Result" blocks are invisible to text ranking and AI overviews.

FixAdd: process/approach, a real testimonial, an author box, an FAQ, and convert the image-only Solution/Result into real HTML text (keep the image as support).

4. Images

You specifically asked about missing alt/title.

PassAlt & title: present on all 129 images

OKEvery <img> has both alt and title. Width/height and loading/fetchpriority are set — good for CLS and LCP.

MediumSame image reused for 6 different "features" + 129 images is heavy

ProblemastrologerAdmin.webp is used as the visual for Astrologer Management, Order & Payment Tracking, Content Management, Reporting, Communication Tools and Fraud Prevention. Several alt texts are also near-duplicated (8× "Astrotalk App Look Like"). 129 images is a large DOM.

WhyRe-using one image for six distinct features is a low-effort signal and confuses users; duplicate alt text adds no value. The image volume (most are the shared tech-logo grid) inflates payload and can hurt LCP/INP on mobile.

FixGive each feature its own visual (or icon) with a unique, descriptive alt. Lazy-load below-the-fold and consider trimming the tech-logo wall.

5. Internal & external linking

HighAlmost no in-content internal links — and none to a money page

ProblemInside the body the only contextual links are to 4 other case studies. There is no in-content link to your astrology-app-development service page or to the related blog /blog/astrology-app-development (which only appears in the sitewide footer).

WhyInternal links pass relevance and authority and tell Google how pages relate. A deep case-study URL with no contextual links in or out is effectively an island — little equity flows to it, and it can't pass equity to your commercial pages. This is a major reason a "long-existing" page stays invisible.

Fix(1) Add contextual body links from this page to your astrology-app service/cost page using descriptive anchors. (2) More importantly, link into this case study from related blogs, the services page and the main case-study hub with anchor text like "app like Astrotalk". Build the internal cluster.

LowNo relevant external / authority links

ProblemThe body cites a market stat ("$2.3 billion by 2028") with no source and links to no authoritative reference.

WhyCiting sources for claims is an E-E-A-T and trust signal, especially for stats. Unsourced numbers read as invented.

FixCite the market-size claim to a named research source (and verify the figure). One or two relevant outbound citations is healthy.

6. Is the URL user- & SEO-friendly?

Mostly yes/case-study/astrotalk-like-app

VerdictYes — it is clean, lowercase, hyphenated, short, readable, contains the keyword "astrotalk-like-app", has no parameters or dates, and the /case-study/ folder signals the content type. Both users and search engines can read it. Keep it.

NoteThe only nuance: searchers more often type "app like astrotalk" or "astrotalk clone" than "astrotalk like app". The URL is good enough — do not change it (a change forces redirects and loses any existing equity). Instead, capture those variants in the title, H1 and body, not the slug.

7. UX

MediumLogo-as-hero, weak scannability, two big lead forms

ProblemThe hero leads with the Astrotalk logo and no descriptive headline; image-only Solution/Result blocks; a swipe-only feature carousel ("Swipe to show features") that hides most features behind interaction; and two near-identical lead forms plus an exit popup.

WhyUsers (and crawlers) get little immediate text telling them what this page offers or what to do. Swipe-gated content is low-discoverability on desktop and for crawlers. Multiple forms + popup can feel pushy and add layout/interaction cost (INP).

FixAdd a clear text headline + sub-line + primary CTA in the hero; expose key features as crawlable text (not swipe-only); make Solution/Result text-first; keep one well-placed lead form.
