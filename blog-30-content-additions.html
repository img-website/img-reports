+ MVP vs Full Build: What to Spend First

Most society management apps fail not on cost but on scope. Splitting the spend into an MVP and a phase-2 full build keeps your first release lean and gets real societies onboarded before you fund the expensive features.

MVP (8-10 weeks, ~INR 5,00,000-8,00,000 / $6,000-9,500): resident + guard + admin logins, visitor pre-approval with OTP and QR gate pass, maintenance bill view and online payment via Razorpay, notice board, complaint tickets, and push notifications. Full build (additional 10-14 weeks, +INR 7,00,000-12,00,000 / +$8,500-14,500): facility/amenity booking with payment, multi-society and multi-tower support, accounting and GST-ready invoicing, vendor and staff attendance, parking management, SOS alerts, in-app community marketplace, and an analytics dashboard for managers.

For context, MyGate itself launched with little more than digital visitor entry and scaled features over years. A practical rule we give clients: spend no more than 40% of your total budget on the MVP, validate with two or three live societies, then reinvest based on which modules residents and committees actually open daily.

+ Hidden and Ongoing Costs of a Society Management App
WHERE TO PLACE Just after the existing H2 'Timeline for Developing a Society Management App'.

The build quote is the down payment, not the lifetime cost. These recurring line items decide whether your app stays profitable after launch, and they are routinely left out of cheap quotes.

  • Cloud hosting (AWS/GCP): INR 8,000-40,000/month ($100-480) depending on number of societies, image/video storage for visitor photos, and notification volume.
  • Payment gateway fees: Razorpay/Stripe charge roughly 2% per maintenance transaction; at scale this is a real margin line, not a rounding error.
  • SMS/OTP and WhatsApp: INR 0.12-0.20 per SMS OTP and per-conversation WhatsApp pricing add up fast with daily visitor approvals.
  • Push notifications: free on FCM, but managed tiers (OneSignal) start around $9-99/month as device counts grow.
  • App store accounts: Apple Developer $99/year, Google Play $25 one-time.
  • Maps/geofencing and facial-recognition APIs: usage-based; budget $50-300/month once enabled.
  • Annual Maintenance Contract (AMC): plan 15-20% of the original build cost per year for OS updates, security patches, bug fixes, and minor enhancements.

A realistic year-one running cost for a small multi-society deployment is INR 3,00,000-7,00,000 ($3,600-8,500) on top of development. Quote this to investors up front so the app does not stall three months after launch.

+ How a Society Management App Recovers Its Cost: Monetization Math
WHERE TO PLACE After the 'iOS vs Android vs Cross-Platform' section and before the existing 'How IMG Global Infotech Can Help' section.

Builders rarely ask only what it costs; they ask how fast it pays back. A society management app has unusually clean unit economics because billing is recurring and the buyer (the RWA or builder) has a fixed budget line.

The dominant model in India is per-flat SaaS pricing. Vendors typically charge INR 8-25 per flat per month. Work the math: a 200-flat society at INR 15/flat = INR 3,000/month, or INR 36,000/year per society. Sign 50 societies and you are at INR 18,00,000 ARR, which clears a INR 12-19 lakh build inside the first 12-18 months.

Other stacked revenue lines:

  • Payment processing markup on maintenance collection (a few basis points on millions in monthly dues).
  • Premium add-ons: accounting/GST reports, facial recognition, advanced analytics, billed per-society.
  • White-label licensing to builders who want their own branded app for new projects, typically a one-time INR 1-3 lakh setup plus annual licence.
  • In-app marketplace and local-vendor ad placements once daily active usage is healthy.

Keep two numbers in front of every investor conversation: customer acquisition cost per society and net revenue retention. With per-flat pricing and sticky maintenance workflows, churn is low and a well-run platform reaches operating break-even well before the typical 18-month consumer-app mark.

+ RBI Digital Lending Compliance: The Cost Factor Everyone Underestimates
WHERE TO PLACE After the new 'Loan App Development Cost Breakdown by Module' section and before 'What Factors Impact the Cost of Loan App Development?'

For an India-based loan app, regulatory compliance is not a checkbox you tick at the end. It shapes your architecture from day one, and ignoring it is the fastest way to get pulled from the Play Store or flagged by the RBI. This is also the line item missing from almost every cheap quote.

Under the RBI Digital Lending Guidelines, your app must lend only through a regulated entity. If you are not an NBFC or bank yourself, you need a lending partner, and the loan flows must run through their books, not yours. Practically, that means building for these requirements:

  • NBFC / bank partnership integration so disbursal and collection move through the regulated entity's accounts, with no pooling in an unregulated middle layer.
  • Default Loss Guarantee (DLG/FLDG) handling, capped at 5% of the loan portfolio, with the logic and reporting baked into your loan management system.
  • Key Fact Statement (KFS) generation showing the all-in Annual Percentage Rate before the borrower accepts, plus a mandatory cooling-off period.
  • Account Aggregator (AA) integration to pull bank statements through an RBI-licensed AA pipe instead of scraping or asking for net-banking credentials.
  • DPDP Act 2023 data handling: explicit consent, data localisation, and a clean audit trail for every piece of borrower data.

Budget-wise, compliance work realistically adds 20% to 40% on top of your base development cost. On an INR 30L build, that is another INR 6L-12L for the legal-engineering, AA onboarding, KFS logic, consent architecture, and the security review (VAPT) that lending apps must pass. A reasonable rule is to set aside 15% to 20% of the initial build cost per year for maintenance and updates alone, before traffic-based costs.

Does the 28% GST apply to my fantasy app?

Yes. Since October 2023, a flat 28% GST is charged on the full amount users deposit into their wallet, not just your platform fee. This must be built into your pricing and accounting from day one.

Can I launch in every Indian state?

No. Real-money fantasy contests are restricted in states including Andhra Pradesh, Telangana, Assam, Odisha, Nagaland and Sikkim. You must geo-fence users in those states.

Is an MVP enough to start earning?

Yes. A compliant cricket-only MVP with KYC, wallet, and live scoring can legally accept paid contests, validate your market, and generate revenue while you fund the full multi-sport build from cash flow.

Do I need a gaming licence?

India has no single national fantasy licence, but you need a sound legal opinion, GST and TDS registration, and adherence to state laws and self-regulatory bodies before going live.
