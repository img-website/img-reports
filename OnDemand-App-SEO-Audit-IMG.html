Evidence The head includes "@type":"Product" with "aggregateRating": { "ratingValue":"4.7","ratingCount":"143" } and an empty image.
Cause Templated from a product-style page; you are a service company, not a product seller.

5. Content quality — generic vs. organic

You asked specifically: is the content generic? In several places — yes. The page has good bones and length, but the prose leans salesy/templated and has a couple of real defects.

P0 Misplaced “taxi” card inside the “Why choose us” section

Evidence In “Why Select IMG Global Infotech for On-Demand App Development Services”, the first card’s heading is “Navigating Taxi Booking App” with taxi-only copy (“…easy time for passengers as well as drivers… our taxi app solution…”). This string appears on this page only — it was pasted from the taxi page and the heading never got changed.
Why it matters On a hub page it reads as a copy-paste error and weakens trust + topical coherence.
Fix Replace with a real hub-level differentiator, e.g. “Multi-vertical on-demand expertise” or “Three-panel architecture (customer · provider · admin)” with matching copy.

P1 Generic / hyperbolic / grammatically broken copy

Hero: “…showcase your thoughts to your audience by the on demand delivery app development company.” — grammatically broken & keyword-stuffed.
“At IMG Global Infotech, we don’t make apps; we just transform your ideas… to achieve crazy results that you never expected before.” — vague, salesy, not credible.
“An on-demand app development company always empowers businesses with their personalized app solutions that can cater to the needs of your business.” — circular filler.
Cause Template prose written for keyword density, not for a human reader.
Fix Rewrite intros to be specific and human: real proof (1,200+ projects, 100+ team, 10+ yrs), concrete capabilities (real-time tracking, dispatch, surge pricing, multi-vendor, wallet, 3-panel apps), and named outcomes. Remove repeated “on demand … app development company” padding — vary phrasing.

P1 Unsourced statistics (E-E-A-T & accuracy risk)

Evidence Stat cards claim $320B Market Size by 2033, 250B+ App Downloads, 8.58% Annual CAGR — with no source.
Why it matters You said “don’t state anything wrong.” Unattributed numbers are an E-E-A-T liability and may simply be inaccurate.
Fix Cite a reputable source + year (Statista / Grand View Research / Business Research Insights) next to each figure, or remove them. Linked, sourced stats also improve AI-citation odds.
