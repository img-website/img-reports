💰 Why this page has never ranked — root causes (with WHY)

1. Highest-tier YMYL niche but zero financial E-E-A-T signalsCritical
Why: Lending sits at the very top of Google's YMYL list — money decisions that affect a user's life. Quality Raters demand auditable expertise: NBFC license / regulator linkage, RBI Digital Lending Guidelines compliance, PCI-DSS certification, named CISO/CTO with photo, ISO 27001 / SOC 2 badges, third-party security audit references. This page shows none of these. For YMYL Finance, this alone caps the page's maximum possible rank well below page 1.
Fix: Add a "Trust & Compliance" section near the top with HIPAA-of-finance equivalents: RBI Digital Lending Guidelines (Sep 2022), PCI-DSS Level 1, ISO 27001, SOC 2 Type II, GDPR, India's DPDP Act 2023. Add a "Reviewed by" block with named compliance officer + photo + LinkedIn.
2. Self-serving aggregateRating 4.7/143 in Product schemaCritical
Why: The page injects Product schema with aggregateRating 4.7 over 143 reviews — but the page itself shows no review block, no per-review markup, no testimonials section even. Google's "self-serving reviews" policy explicitly forbids merchants from adding aggregated review markup not visible to users. This is one of the most common causes of structured-data manual actions and silent algorithmic suppression. Worse, the Product schema also has "image":"" (empty string), which is invalid and will throw a Rich Results error.
Fix: Drop the Product schema entirely. The Service schema already covers the offering. If you keep Product, set a valid image URL and remove aggregateRating until you have a visible, populated review widget on the page with per-review markup.
3. Invalid FAQ JSON-LD (raw newlines inside string)Critical
Why: The FAQ schema's first text value contains raw line breaks and tabs without JSON escaping: 
"text": "Here are some steps to develop a loan app
            Market Research
            Proper Planning ..."
Raw newlines inside a JSON string value are invalid JSON. Google's parser will either drop this FAQ from the eligible set or, in stricter cases, ignore the entire FAQ block. Validator tools (Rich Results Test) will flag this as a parse error.
Fix: Replace newlines with \n or with HTML <br> entities. Better still, rewrite each FAQ answer as one well-structured paragraph in a single line. Then validate with Google Rich Results Test.
4. Schema duplication — Service AND Product describe the same offeringHigh
Why: The same page declares the offering as Service (correct) and as Product (incorrect for a B2B service). Google can pick the wrong one. Worse, the two contradict each other on properties — the brand in Product points to IMG (your brand isn't the product), while provider in Service correctly points to IMG. Combined with the fake aggregateRating, this looks like classic structured-data spam.
Fix: Keep Organization, WebSite, WebPage, Service, BreadcrumbList, and FAQPage. Delete the Product block.
5. Brand misuse in H2 — "Like MoneyView and Lazypay"Critical
Why: Hero-adjacent H2 reads "Create Your Own App for Instant Loans Like MoneyView and Lazypay". Both are live, registered Indian NBFC-backed lending brands. Three problems:
  • Trademark / DMCA exposure — these brands have legal teams that file regular take-downs.
  • Google increasingly demotes pages that piggyback on competitor brand SERPs (the "clone app" pattern).
  • Builds zero topical authority for "loan lending app development" because the H2 is about a competitor, not about your service.
Fix: Rewrite to focus on outcomes, not competitor names: "Build a Compliant Instant Loan App for Indian and Global Markets". If you need a clone-app comparison, give it its own clearly-labeled "Apps inspired by leading lenders" sub-block lower on the page.
6. Copy-paste leftovers in case-study block (the Pocketly logo file)Critical
Why: The "Pocketly" case study card pulls its logo from loanLandingAppEventTicketsPlusLogo.webp — a filename clearly belonging to an event-tickets app, not a lending app. This is a smoking-gun copy-paste leftover from a template page, confirming the case studies were not built bespoke for this URL. Combined with the unverifiable claim "We develop powerful instant loan apps like NextFin/Pocketly", Google's content classifier reads this as a templated, low-quality page. For YMYL, that's a hard pass.
Fix: Replace with real, named IMG case studies — client logo, client name, real metrics, named contact for reference (with permission). If real cases don't exist publicly, remove the section entirely. Half-fabricated case studies are worse than no case studies on YMYL pages.
7. Zero regulatory / compliance content for lendingCritical
Why: The page mentions "data security, payment integration, AI-based credit scoring" in passing, but nowhere covers the actual regulatory frame a buyer needs to comply with: RBI Digital Lending Guidelines (Sep 2022), DLG / FLDG rules, KYC/AML/CFT, eKYC via Aadhaar/CKYC, eSign / NSDL, credit bureau integration (CIBIL, Experian, Equifax, CRIF), NACH / eNACH for repayment, Account Aggregator framework (NPCI), Cooling-Off Period, Key Fact Statement (KFS), grievance redressal.
