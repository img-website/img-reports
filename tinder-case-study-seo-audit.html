1. The root cause — why it has never ranked

Most important section.

CriticalOptimised for the brand "Tinder", not a keyword you can win

ProblemTitle + H1 + OG all say Tinder – Transforming Online Dating with Smart Matching. That's what Tinder's own site ranks for. An agency case study can't outrank the actual product for its brand name. The commercial phrase ("Build your own dating app") sits only in the meta description, not the title/H1.

WhyGoogle ranks on intent. "Tinder" searchers want the app; your buyers search "dating app development", "build an app like Tinder", "Tinder clone app cost". None of those are in the title/H1, so you rank for nothing winnable.

FixRe-target to commercial intent:
Title → Tinder Like App Development | Build a Dating App Like Tinder — IMG Global Infotech
Visible H1 → Tinder Like App Development — Case Study. Keep "Tinder" as context; make "dating app development / like app" the head term.

CriticalThin + near-duplicate template across 12 case studies

ProblemThis page is one of the thinnest: short Business Goal / Project Idea / Challenges paragraphs, only a User App + Admin Panel feature set, and the same shared "Cutting Edge Technologies" logo grid + "AI-Powered Features" block + "Proposed Talent & Time" copy as Astrotalk, Uber, Zillow, Netflix, etc.

WhyHelpful-Content and duplicate systems demote templated, low-depth pages; 12 lookalikes dilute each other so none becomes the authority for its topic. Thin unique content gives Google little reason to rank this over a competitor's deeper "develop a dating app like Tinder" page.

FixMake ≥50% of the body unique to dating apps: real build story, architecture (geolocation matching, swipe engine, real-time chat, moderation/safety, fake-profile detection), screenshots, defensible outcomes. Push the shared logo grid to a secondary block.

2. Content / labels that do NOT belong on this page

You asked: kya kisi aur page ka content/label yahan likha hai? Yes — one clear case.

HighAI-features image alt says "Practo-Like App" on the Tinder page

ProblemThe heading reads "AI-Powered Features That Make Your Tinder-Like App Stand Out", but the image right under it has leftover Practo labels:

![AI-Powered Features That Make Your Practo-Like App Stand Out](…/tinderAiPowered.webp "AI-Powered Features That Make Your Practo-Like App Stand Out")

WhyAlt/title text is read by search engines and image search. Telling Google this image is about "Practo" on a Tinder page scrambles topical relevance and proves the section was copied from the Practo template without cleanup — the same duplication signal from §1, made visible.

FixCorrect the alt/title to match the page: alt="AI-powered features for a dating app like Tinder". Audit the other case studies for the same leftover.

LowCompetitor naming & keyword nudges in body

Problem"Apps like OkCupid are its strong competitors, but when it comes to it, Tinder is paramount." and "…find their perfect match on the dating platform app like Tinder." (keyword bolted onto a feature line).

WhyNaming OkCupid is acceptable context, but "when it comes to it, Tinder is paramount" is filler, and the bolted "app like Tinder" reads as a mild keyword nudge rather than natural writing.

FixTighten the language; drop filler; place the keyword once where it fits naturally.

3. Content quality & the "Result" section

Your brief: real, organic, human content with proof — verified.

HighThe "Result" is vague puffery with zero metrics

ProblemThe entire Result section:

"As a result, the app is on the top charts and exceeded our clients' expectations. It has completely transformed the online dating experience for users and established a strong brand image among its competitors."

"Our Solution" is similarly generic ("a sleek dating app with a stunning interface and robust backend").

WhyA case study earns trust and links through specific, verifiable outcomes — downloads, matches/day, retention, time-to-market, revenue. "On the top charts" with no number is unprovable marketing language; it doesn't differentiate you and gives Google/users nothing concrete.

FixReplace with real metrics (e.g. "X downloads in 3 months", "Y% D30 retention", "MVP shipped in 12 weeks", "handled Z concurrent users"). If confidential, use honest ranges.

MediumThin body + minor awkward phrasing

ProblemUnique copy is short (a few sentences per section). Small awkward idioms: "the rest of the history is in front of the world", "counted as one of the sensational dating apps". Team shows "01 App Developer" for a full dating app.

WhyLow word-depth + a couple of non-native idioms reduce both ranking potential (not enough substance) and polish. A one-developer team line can undercut credibility for a complex app.

FixExpand each section with concrete detail; smooth the idioms; present the team realistically.

4. Missing & weak sections

MediumFAQ schema injected, but no FAQ section on the page

Problem@include('tailwind.schema.faqSchema') runs, but no visible FAQ block renders (no DB FAQ rows for this URL).

WhyYou lose easy long-tail capture ("cost to build a dating app like Tinder", "how long to develop a dating app"), AND FAQ structured data with no matching visible content violates Google policy and risks a manual action.

FixAdd a real FAQ section (cost, timeline, features, monetisation, moderation) and populate the FAQ DB row so schema = content.

MediumNo author/E-E-A-T, no testimonial, image-led Solution/Result

ProblemNo author byline, no client quote; "Our Solution"/"Result" are short text + image with little crawlable substance.

WhyExpertise + social-proof signals drive trust and ranking for commercial content; thin image-led sections add little extractable text for ranking or AI overviews.

FixAdd an author/expert box, a genuine testimonial, and a process/approach section with real text.

5. Technical & on-page SEO

CriticalH1 is hidden and keyword-less

ProblemOnly H1 is <h1 class="hidden">Tinder - Case Study</h1>. The visible top is a logo image.

WhyThe H1 is a primary relevance signal; hiding it with a generic value wastes it and leaves no visible keyword-rich heading for users or crawlers.

FixOne visible H1 with the target phrase (Tinder Like App Development — Case Study) + a supporting line.

MediumNo Article / CreativeWork / Service schema

ProblemOnly Breadcrumb + (empty) FAQ schema attempted; nothing describes the case study, author, client or service.

WhyRich structured data helps Google map agency → service → outcome and improves eligibility for enhanced results / AI citations.

FixAdd Article/CreativeWork (author + dates) and Service schema linking to your dating-app-development service page.

LowWrong geo meta + unsourced stats

Problemmeta geo.region = DE (Germany) on a global page; "75 million users / 9.7 million subscribers / $2.1 billion revenue" are unsourced.

WhyWrong geo is a minor mis-signal; unsourced stats weaken trust/E-E-A-T.

FixSet geo to the intended market (or remove); cite and verify the stats.

6. Images

PassAlt & title present on all 115 images

OKEvery <img> has both alt and title; width/height + loading/fetchpriority set — good for CLS/LCP.

MediumOne wrong alt (Practo) + heavy image count + likely reuse

ProblemThe AI-features alt is "Practo-Like App" (see §2); 115 images is a heavy DOM (mostly the shared logo grid); admin features likely reuse one graphic as on the other case studies.

WhyWrong alt mis-describes content; image volume inflates payload and can hurt mobile LCP/INP; reused images are a low-effort signal.

FixFix the Practo alt, give each feature a unique image + alt, lazy-load below-the-fold, and trim the logo wall.

7. Internal & external linking

HighNo in-content link to a dating-app service/money page

ProblemIn-body contextual links go only to 4 other case studies. There's a "Latest Insights" blog block, but no contextual link to your dating-app-development service or a cost blog from within the unique content.

WhyInternal links pass relevance and authority and route users toward conversion. A deep case-study URL with no contextual links in or out is an island — little equity reaches it, and it passes none to your commercial pages.

Fix(1) Add contextual body links to your dating-app-development service + cost pages with descriptive anchors. (2) Link into this case study from that service page, related blogs and the case-study hub using anchors like "app like Tinder".

8. Is the URL user- & SEO-friendly?

Mostly yes/case-study/tinder-like-app

VerdictYes — clean, lowercase, hyphenated, short, readable, keyword present ("tinder-like-app"), no parameters/dates, and /case-study/ signals content type. Keep it.

NotePeople also search "dating app like Tinder" / "Tinder clone" — capture those in the title, H1 and body, not the slug. Don't change the URL (a change forces redirects and risks losing equity).

9. UX

MediumLogo-as-hero, swipe-gated features, thin Result, duplicate forms

ProblemHero leads with the Tinder logo and no descriptive headline; features hide behind a "Swipe to show features" carousel; Solution/Result are short + image-led; two near-identical lead forms + exit popup.

WhyLittle immediate text tells users/crawlers what the page offers; swipe-gated content is low-discoverability; thin proof + multiple forms/popup raise interaction cost (INP) and feel pushy.

FixAdd a clear text headline + sub-line + primary CTA in the hero; expose features as crawlable text; make Solution/Result text-first with metrics; keep one well-placed form.
